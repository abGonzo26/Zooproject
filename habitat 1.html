Savanna

savanna, also spelled savannah, vegetation type that grows under hot, seasonally dry climatic conditions and is characterized by an open tree canopy (i.e., scattered trees) above a continuous tall grass understory (the vegetation layer between the forest canopy and the ground).

In the first Habitat we are going to be talking about two different types of animals.
We are going to be talking about The king of the grasslands and the fastest animal in the world, The Lion and the Cheetah

The KING of the GRASSLANDS: LION

The lion is a large cat of the genus Panther native to Africa and India.
It has a muscular, broad-chested body; short, rounded head; round ears; and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail.
It is sexually dimorphic; adult male lions are larger than females and have a prominent mane.

The fastest animal in the world: Cheetah

The cheetah is a large cat native to Africa, central Iran and India. It is the fastest land animal, capable of running at 80 to 98 km/h; as such, it has evolved specialized adaptations for speed, including a light build, long thin legs and a long tail.
